Taking Care of Your Mind

We all face tough days. The key is learning to notice when things feel off—and knowing small steps can help.
Below, you’ll find calming techniques and signs that may signal it’s time to pause, reflect, or reach out. You’re not alone.

Practical Tips

Focus on the present moment using your senses—describe five things you see, touch, hear, smell, or taste. It helps reduce racing thoughts.

Try box breathing: inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold again for 4. Repeat to calm your nervous system.

Let your thoughts flow freely onto paper—even if it’s messy. It helps externalize emotions and create space for clarity.

When emotions feel overwhelming, try labeling them—like “anxious,” “sad,” or “confused.” Identifying a feeling helps reduce its intensity and gives you space to respond gently.
